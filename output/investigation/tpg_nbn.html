      Using your own modem with TPG

      Check your modem is compatible

      You’ll need to verify that your modem is compatible with TPG at your address. TPG will only be able to provide general support on your service – and you’ll need to contact the modem manufacturer directly for any technical support needed.

      Understand your tech type

      If you have FTTN, FTTB or FTTC at your address, your BYO modem will need to be VDSL2 compatible. Make sure you check your modem device guide to see if your modem supports PPPoE and is VDSL2 compatible.

      Consider a TPG modem for streamlined support

      If you use a TPG-supplied modem and need support, our team is available to help. In many cases, we’ll be able to connect to your modem remotely and help troubleshoot your service.

      Do I have to switch to nbn®?

      If you live in an area where nbn® has been rolled out and you want to keep your landline or internet service, you need to switch to nbn® before the copper-based services in your area are disabled. This may occur about 18 months after nbn® becomes available in your area.

      You don’t need to switch to nbn® if you live in an area where nbn® Fixed Wireless is available. Copper-based services will not be disabled in Fixed Wireless areas, however we recommend switching and enjoy the speeds of nbn®.

      What are the different nbn® connection types?

      TPG delivers the following nbn® connection types, depending on your location:

          • Fibre to the Premises (FTTP): fibre-optic cable from a fibre node directly to the premises, e.g., house or business.

          • Fibre to the Node (FTTN): fibre-optic cable to a neighbourhood node, and from there, using the existing copper network to the premises.

          • Fibre to the Building (FTTB): fibre-optic cable to the basement of multi-dwelling units such as apartment blocks and from there using the existing copper wire to the premises.

          • Fibre to the Curb (FTTC): fibre-optic cable to an outdoor pit near the premises and from there using the existing copper network to the premises.

          • Cable/Hybrid Fibre Coaxial (HFC): a combination of fibre and coaxial cable.

          • Fixed Wireless: radio communications from an nbn® Fixed Wireless facility directly to a small outdoor antenna attached to your premises.

      How long does installation take?

      Installation of an nbn® service generally takes less than 10 business days but can take longer in some cases. This depends on the demand of nbn® services in your area and the availability of a technician to perform the installation.

      1. Schedule an appointment

        If your premises had an active nbn® service connected previously, then you likely won’t require a technician’s visit.

        If installation is required, TPG will organise the appointment for you. You or an authorised person over 18 years of age will need to be at the premises on the day of the technician visit.

      2. Before your installation appointment

        Identify where you want the nbn® equipment to be installed. The ideal area is:

            • close to an accessible power point;
            • cool, dry and ventilated; and
            • away from busy areas where the equipment may be knocked and damaged.

        The installer will confirm whether the requested work can be performed without additional charges.

      3. What to expect during installation

        If your building doesn’t already have a fibre-optic cable connected, running the cable into your premises may require digging a small trench, or, if it’s coming from overhead, some minor clearing of vegetation. nbn® has a strict "leave it as it's found" policy so there won’t be any damage to your property.

        Furniture may need to be moved and some drilling work might be required to mount the equipment, so be prepared for noise and dust. Some locations might not be possible due to safety or other considerations such as if there is additional wiring work required.

        If your premises already have fibre-optic cable connected to a Premises Connection Device (PCD), standard installations normally take 2–4 hours.

        If your premises don’t have the fibre optic cable connected, a standard installation could take 4–8 hours. If you request a non-standard installation, the time required may be longer.

      4. What nbn® equipment will be installed?

        If you select the modem, TPG will provide you with a high-speed Wi-Fi 7 modem with VoIP capabilities that will let you connect to the internet and make/receive phone calls using your TPG Voice Service.

        Your nbn® installer will physically connect the FTTC connection to the nbn® network and install the NBN® Connection Device (NCD) in your premises.

          • NBN® Utility Box or Premises Connection Device (PCD) 
          • NBN® Connection Box or Network Termination Device (NTD)

      TPG Voice Services

      What is TPG Voice Service?

      TPG Voice Service on NBN FTTN, FTTB, FTTC, Cable (HFC) and Fixed Wireless is a Digital Voice Service which is delivered using Voice over Internet Protocol (VoIP) technology. Digital Voice Service requires a broadband connection and power to the Wi-Fi modem router supplied by TPG. If there is a broadband connection outage in your premises due to power or other faults, the Digital Voice Service will not work, and you cannot make phone calls including "000" emergency calls.

      Once an nbn® Bundle plan is purchased, you cannot separately cancel nbn® Broadband or Voice Service. Cancellation will cease both services. You cannot purchase Voice Service as a standalone service.

      What does TPG Voice Service support?

      TPG Voice Service supports: fax to national numbers in the Telstra, Optus, AAPT and Primus PSTN network.

      TPG Voice Service doesn’t support:

          • fax to international numbers;

          • Priority Assistance Scheme;

          • analogue modems;

          • calls to 19/1900, 0500 numbers and Universal International Freephone Numbers (UIFN); or

          • old dial-up technology devices with built in modems, such as EFTPOS, Back-to-Base Alarms and Foxtel Program purchases. These devices have been replaced by Internet Protocol (IP) enabled devices. For further details on IP-enabled devices contact your device supplier.

      Can I port my number to TPG?

      You can port (transfer) your phone number from Telstra, Optus, AAPT, Primus or Powertel to TPG. We may not be able to port your number in some circumstances, including where you fail to provide correct account details, or as a result of contractual obligations with your existing provider.

      TPG can also supply a new number, which will be allocated based on your registered address. TPG will supply the Integrated Public Number Database (IPND) with your address details for emergency services (000) calling.

      If you are porting your number to TPG, you can still make outbound calls but inbound calling to your number will not commence until porting is complete, which will take 1–5 business days.

      Will my phone service work in a power outage?

      TPG nbn® services on FTTN, FTTB, FTTC, HFC and Fixed Wireless technologies will NOT work in the event of a power outage. The Voice Service requires a broadband connection and power to the Wi-Fi modem supplied by TPG. If there is a broadband connection outage in your premises due to power or other faults, the Digital Voice Service will not work and you cannot make phone calls including "000" emergency calls.

      Do I need a backup power service?

      nbn® services require a backup battery unit to keep power going to your nbn® Network Termination Device in the event of a power outage, so that some elements of your telephone service can continue to operate for about 5 hours during the power outage.

      TPG nbn® service does not include the supply of a backup battery unit. This means during a power outage, you will not be able to make or receive any calls, including calls to emergency services. Please ensure that you have an alternative method to make calls such as a mobile phone.
